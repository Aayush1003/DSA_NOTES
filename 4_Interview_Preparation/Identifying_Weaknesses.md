# How to Identify Your DSA Gaps (Guidance)

To truly master DSA, you need to know *what* you don't know. Use this checklist to find your "lacking" areas.

## 1. The 3 Levels of Mastery
For every topic (e.g., Graphs, DP, Heaps), ask yourself:
- **Level 1 (Concept):** Can I explain how it works in 3 sentences?
- **Level 2 (Implementation):** Can I code the basic structure (e.g., BFS, QuickSort) from scratch in 5 minutes without looking?
- **Level 3 (Application):** Can I solve a "Medium" problem on this topic within 30 minutes?

**Action:** If you are at Level 1 or 2, don't move to Level 3. Re-read the notes in `2_Data_Structures` or `3_Algorithms`.

## 2. Common "Lacking" Areas
Check if any of these describe you:
- [ ] **"I can solve it but it's slow":** You lack knowledge of **Complexity Analysis**. Read `1_Basics_And_Complexity`.
- [ ] **"I don't know which pattern to use":** You lack **Pattern Recognition**. Read `4_Interview_Preparation/Pattern_Identification_Guide.md`.
- [ ] **"I get stuck on recursion/indices":** You lack **Implementation Precision**. Practice small, focused problems (e.g., Two Sum, Reverse Linked List) until they are muscle memory.
- [ ] **"I freeze when the interviewer asks a question":** You lack **Communication Skills**. Read `4_Interview_Preparation/Interview_Communication_Framework.md`.

## 3. The "Weakness Log"
Create a file (or use `Guides/Problem_Template.md`) and specifically record:
1. **Topic:** (e.g., Dynamic Programming)
2. **Why I failed:** (e.g., "Couldn't define the state", "Index out of bounds error")
3. **The 'Aha!' moment:** What was the one piece of information that made it click?

## 4. Guidance for the "Plateau"
If you've solved 100+ problems but still feel "lucky" when you solve one:
- **Stop solving new problems.**
- **Re-solve old problems** using a different approach (e.g., solve a Recursion problem with Iteration).
- **Explain the problem to someone else.** If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it deeply enough.

---
*Remember: It's better to solve 50 problems and understand the patterns perfectly than to solve 500 problems by memorizing solutions.*
