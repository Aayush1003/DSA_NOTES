# AI Learning Companion: How to Use AI for DSA

To master DSA in 2024+, you shouldn't just "Google" or "Copy-Paste". You should use AI as a high-level mentor. Use these prompts and strategies.

## 1. The "Deep Understanding" Prompts
When you don't understand a concept (e.g., Dijkstra), don't just ask for code. Ask:
- *"Explain Dijkstra's algorithm to me using a real-world analogy involving a delivery truck."*
- *"Why is a Priority Queue used in Dijkstra instead of a simple Queue? What happens if we swap them?"*
- *"Show me a step-by-step trace of this algorithm on a small graph with 4 nodes."*

## 2. The "Stuck" Strategy (No-Spoilers)
If you've been stuck for 30 minutes, don't ask for the solution. Ask the AI for a **nudge**:
- *"I'm trying to solve 'Number of Islands'. I'm using DFS but I'm getting a StackOverflow error. Give me a hint without showing the full code."*
- *"Can you review my approach? I'm using a Hash Map to store X. Is there a more space-efficient way?"*

## 3. The "Edge Case" Finder
Before you submit your code, ask the AI to break it:
- *"Here is my solution for 'Merge Intervals'. Can you identify 3 edge cases where this might fail or be inefficient?"*
- *"What happens if the input is an empty list or contains duplicate ranges?"*

## 4. The "Code Review" for Cleanliness
Once you solve it, ask:
- *"Here is my working code. Can you suggest how to make it more readable or 'industry standard'?"*
- *"What are the trade-offs of my current implementation compared to a recursive one?"*

## 5. Mock Interview with AI
Use this prompt:
- *"I want you to act as a Senior Software Engineer at Google. Give me a 'Medium' difficulty problem related to 'Trees'. Don't give me the solution. Ask me clarifying questions, and let me propose an approach first."*

---
**Warning:** Never memorize AI-generated code. Always type it out yourself and understand every line. AI is your **coach**, not your **ghostwriter**.
